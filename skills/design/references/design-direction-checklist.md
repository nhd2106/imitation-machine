# Design Direction Checklist

Use this before implementation or handoff.

- Audience: who is this for and what context are they in?
- Design direction: can you describe the visual direction without saying only "clean" or "modern"?
- Memorable anchor: what is the one thing users should remember?
- Interaction quality: what should feel especially crisp, calm, fast, dense, or playful?
- Responsive behavior: what changes at mobile, tablet, and desktop?
- States: what happens in loading, empty, error, success, hover, and focus states?
- Constraints: what accessibility, performance, brand, and platform limits shape the work?
- Handoff readiness: could another implementer build this without guessing the taste level?
- Browser validation: which breakpoints, states, and browsers must be checked before done?
